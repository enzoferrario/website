    Psychology

    One of the things I like learning about is the human mind and how it works on the inside. The brain is incredible and research has shown that there are still interesting concepts that can be researched today. Specifically, I enjoy human behavior and analysis of the mind. Humans are really weird in how we act. A lot of the time, humans follow the same patterns as each other. Same habits, same line of thinking, generally a lot is the same with most people. Both passively and actively, the mind is great for observation. For example, most people tend to look at people's eyes when their walking through a crowd. If they see that someone is looking one way, the brain picks up 2 things: they don't see where I'm going and they are going that way, so we instictively get out of their way. The opposite is true as well. If, when you're walking through a crowd, you look straight ahead, people most of the time will let you pass.

    Parkour

    I've always enjoyed playing sports, but none as much as parkour. Parkour as a hobby is attractive to be becaues it's so free and fluid. It's also attractive because it's never the same. In my own life, I don't do a whole lot of stylish parkour; I prefer to stick to freerunning. The difference between the two is that the goal of freerunning is to get from point A to point B as fast as you can in any way possible. Vaults, climbing, rolls, various mechanics are different than that of regular parkour. Freerunners make their own paths and sometimes even their own movement. Its a lot of fun to do myself and to teach the basics to others. They seem to enjoy what I teach.

    Comedy

    Less of a hobby, more of an important part of me. I believe that the ability to joke and the ability to take a joke is the most important part of life. If you take life too seriously, you are never going to enjoy it. You will be worked up all the time for seemingly no reason, and it is going to be a lot to handle. A sense of humor will give you the ability to see life through a much lighter lens and allow you to relax and destress. One ability that I needed to learn when I was younger was being able to laugh at myself. Life was stressful for me at a young age because I was so strung up. If anything was said about me, complimentary or not, it would bother me and I would get sad. If I made the smallest mistake, I would begin to cry. Since then, I grew a lot and learned that it's ok to laugh and it's ok to make people laugh. Laughter is the best medicine, as they say.

    I really enjoy making people smile as well. Seeing them happy because of something I said or did brings me a lot of joy. And I can't expect people to laugh at every joke that I tell, and thats ok. I'm my own biggest fan and if other people laugh with me, I consider that a bonus.
